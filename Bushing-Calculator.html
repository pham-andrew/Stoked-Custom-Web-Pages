Welcome to the Stoked Ultimate Bushing Calculator. The goal of this tool is to create the easiest to use bushing durometer calculator for any bushing brand out there. Simply put in your weight to the box and recommended ranges will appear. Click on the unit of weight to change from lbs to kgs.

For reference, most reverse kingpin trucks come with approximately 90a as the stock bushing. Traditional kingpin trucks' bushings may start a little harder.

These are just recommendations, but we feel they are great starting point.

One caveat: These recommendations are for general riding only. For those that want to do specialized riding (such as LDP), the recommendations will be different.

RipTide is the most complete bushing company out there. They have tons of custom shapes and designs for just about every skateboard.

RipTide APS

RipTide APS is the most "normal" bushing in the RipTide line up, but there is hardly anything normal about them. APS is designed to perform. Period. We recommend this bushing to anyone looking to get higher performance out of any skateboard truck.

RipTide APS comes in a wide range of bushing durometers and shapes. They are best used for the general skateboarder, where no unique or special performance needs are required (other than getting gnargnar).

Oust Bushings

Oust bushings are on the pricey-er side but for good reason! Every product that Oust releases is high quality, made in the USA. Uber bushings are manufactured using a blended pre-polymer urethane making them very resilient.

Oust Uber bushings are injection molded at 90,000 PSI making them the most internally consistent bushing. When compressed with 20,000 pounds of pressure for three minutes, they returned to original size after 27 seconds.

Bones Hardcore

Bones hardcore are currently the most popular street skating bushings. As their name suggests they get harder as you near the kingpin creating a "hard core".

The hard core keeps the bushing from being compressed on the the opposite side of turn. It isolates the compression to keep the opposite side from being pushed away from the kingpin.
